# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own quality strategy and execution across projects. They set quality benchmarks, coordinate testing efforts (manual and automated), and collaborate with developers to identify and resolve defects efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and acceptance criteria for testing
- Own and maintain test plans and test strategies
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Triage and track defects through resolution
- Collaborate with developers on test automation and CI/CD integration
- Identify quality risks and propose mitigations
- Ensure release readiness through comprehensive testing

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality features that meet customer expectations
- Minimize defects in production
- Enable fast, confident releases through robust testing practices
- Build a shared quality mindset across the team

### Typical Communication
- Quality status reports in weekly syncs
- Test plans and strategy documents
- Defect triage and escalation to developers and PM
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

### Key Interactions
- Works with **Developers** to design testable code and troubleshoot failures
- Collaborates with **Product Manager** to clarify acceptance criteria and success metrics
- Partners with **Project Manager** to ensure quality gates are part of the delivery timeline
- Coordinates with **UX Designer** on usability testing
- Liaises with **Security Engineer** on security testing and validation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user research, interaction design, and usability standards. They advocate for user needs throughout the project lifecycle and ensure solutions are intuitive and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research to understand needs and pain points
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Define user journey maps and interaction flows
- Set and maintain usability and accessibility standards
- Participate in design reviews and provide feedback
- Validate designs through user testing and feedback loops
- Collaborate on design system and component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered solutions
- Reduce support burden through excellent usability
- Ensure solutions are accessible to all users
- Build empathy for users across the team

### Typical Communication
- Design specs and user research findings
- Wireframes, prototypes, and design artifacts
- Design critique sessions and feedback on mockups
- User testing sessions and usability reports
- Collaboration in kickoff and planning meetings

### Key Interactions
- Works with **Product Manager** to understand user needs and validate solutions
- Collaborates with **Developers** on feasibility and implementation of designs
- Partners with **QA Lead** on usability testing plans
- Coordinates with **Technical Writer** on user-facing documentation
- Engages with **Security Engineer** on secure interaction patterns

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers bring the customer voice into projects. They track adoption, gather feedback, and collaborate with product and project teams to ensure solutions deliver real value and are adopted by users.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback and usage data
- Track feature adoption and customer satisfaction metrics
- Define success criteria and enablement strategies
- Identify customer pain points and escalate to product team
- Support rollout and change management for new features
- Maintain ongoing communication with key customers
- Contribute to retrospectives with customer insights

### Goals
- Ensure features deliver measurable customer value
- Increase feature adoption and customer satisfaction
- Reduce customer risk and escalations
- Build strong customer relationships and trust

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback reports and adoption metrics
- Quarterly business reviews and customer updates
- Feedback loops integrated into sprint retrospectives
- Change management and rollout communications
- Executive summaries for stakeholder updates

### Key Interactions
- Partners with **Product Manager** on priorities and success metrics
- Collaborates with **Project Manager** on rollout planning and timelines
- Works with **Technical Writer** on user enablement materials
- Engages with **Developers** to understand feature capabilities
- Supports **QA Lead** with real-world user scenarios for testing

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers author and maintain user-facing documentation, guides, and enablement materials. They work across all technical roles to ensure knowledge is captured, clear, and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Author user guides, API documentation, and knowledge base articles
- Maintain and version documentation alongside product releases
- Create onboarding and training materials
- Collaborate with technical teams to understand features and use cases
- Edit and refine documentation for clarity and consistency
- Manage documentation structure and searchability
- Contribute to release notes and migration guides

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through comprehensive, clear documentation
- Enable users to self-serve and adopt features independently
- Maintain consistency in terminology and style across all materials
- Improve customer experience and product usability perception

### Typical Communication
- User guides, API docs, and knowledge base articles
- Release notes and migration guides
- Contribution to onboarding materials and training sessions
- Documentation review and feedback from stakeholders
- Participation in sprint planning for documentation scope

### Key Interactions
- Works with **Developers** to understand technical architecture and APIs
- Collaborates with **UX Designer** on user-facing copy and information architecture
- Partners with **Product Manager** on feature announcements and positioning
- Engages with **Customer Success Manager** on enablement strategies
- Supports **QA Lead** with test documentation and terminology
- Coordinates with **Project Manager** on release timing and rollout communications

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers advise on secure architecture, review code and designs for vulnerabilities, and lead incident response. They ensure security is integrated throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Review architecture and designs for security risks
- Conduct code reviews for security vulnerabilities
- Advise on secure coding practices and libraries
- Manage security scanning and testing in CI/CD
- Lead incident response and post-incident reviews
- Maintain threat model and risk assessments
- Provide security guidance during threat escalation

### Goals
- Build secure systems that protect customer data
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and breach risk
- Foster a security-aware culture across teams
- Respond rapidly and transparently to security incidents

### Typical Communication
- Security threat models and architecture reviews
- Vulnerability reports and remediation guidance
- Security scanning results in CI/CD
- Incident notifications and response coordination
- Security training and best practice guidance

### Key Interactions
- Collaborates with **Developers** on secure coding and vulnerability remediation
- Partners with **QA Lead** on security testing and validation plans
- Works with **Project Manager** to escalate risks and manage incident response
- Engages with **Product Manager** on security requirements and trade-offs
- Advises all roles on secure design and implementation practices
